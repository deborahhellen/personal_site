About

Deborah is a life-long horsewoman and dressage rider currently located in Arlington, VA. She grew up riding with Tami Batts in Greensboro, NC and relocated to Massachusetts in 2012 to pursue an engineering degree at Olin College. She received her USDF Bronze Medal in the summer of 2012 and completed the USDF "L" Program with distinction in 2015. She takes pride in introducing horses of all breeds to dressage and promoting the development of happy athletes through correct training and fair judging. Now newly settled in northern VA, she balances a day job as a software engineer with training her own young horse and judging schooling shows on weekends.

Services



Judging


Deborah completed the USDF "L" program with Distinction at age 21 and aspires to become a USEF licensed judge. She is currently available for judging dressage schooling shows in Maryland, Virginia, and surrounding states. Other locations on the east coast may be available, circumstances apply.

Training


At this time Deborah is unable to take horses and riders into full or partial training, but she is happy to schedule a lesson with you on your horse in the greater Washington DC area. Please inquire below for more details on availability!
